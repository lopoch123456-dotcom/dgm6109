Relationship between Date and transaction amount

Hypothesis: As my transaction amount increases, my psychological satisfaction relies more heavily on whether the purchase was a "Spark" (impulse/joy) or "Survival" (necessity), rather than just the cost alone.

Why this is my best scatterplot: I believe this is one of the best scatter plots I've ever created because it successfully integrates the four dimensions of my personal tracking data into a clear and easy-to-read visualization. By placing the date on the X-axis and the amount on the Y-axis, it clearly shows the chronological order of my spending habits. Furthermore, by cleaning the data with filters and organizing the timeline with sort, I added category colors (like "small purchases" and "necessities") and bubble sizes (e.g., satisfaction levels). This multi-layered approach helped me visually identify different clusters, such as whether my highest satisfaction levels came from small "small purchases" or large "necessities."
